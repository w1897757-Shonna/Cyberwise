Relationship Scams
Scammers use romance, friendship, or kindness to gain your trust and steal your money. They may spend weeks, months, or even years pretending the relationship is real.

What is a relationship scam?

A relationship scam happens when someone pretends to care about you online so they can get money, personal details, or access to your accounts.

They may contact you through social media, dating websites, messaging apps, gaming apps, or email. They often create fake profiles and may even use pictures of other people or famous people.

At first, they may seem loving, kind, and very interested in you. They may message or call often to make you feel special. This is sometimes called love bombing.

Warning signs it might be a scam

  • The relationship moves very quickly and they say they have strong feelings for you early on.
  • They message or call a lot to make you feel special or dependent on them.
  • They ask to move the conversation from a dating site or social media platform to WhatsApp, WeChat, LINE, or another messaging app.
  • They ask for money for emergencies, travel, bills, medical problems, or family troubles.
  • They encourage you to invest money, especially in cryptocurrency or “easy money” schemes.
  • They ask you to receive, move, or transfer money for them.
  • They want the relationship kept secret and ask you not to tell family or friends.
  • They try to isolate you from people you trust.
  • They always have an excuse for not meeting in person or video calling.
  • They say they are overseas, in a remote area, or that their camera or internet is not working.
  • Their profile does not match what they tell you about themselves.
  • They ask for personal photos, videos, identity documents, or bank details.
  • They become angry, threatening, or upset if you ask questions or say no.

How to spot a fake profile

  • Photos look too professional, blurry, or unusual.
  • The account is very new.
  • There is very little personal information.
  • There are few friends, followers, comments, or likes.
  • The same photo appears online under different names.

How to protect yourself

  • Never send money to someone you have only met online.
  • Never share bank details, card details, passwords, or identity documents.
  • Never agree to transfer money for someone else. This may involve you in money laundering, which is a crime.
  • Take things slowly. Ask questions and watch for answers that do not make sense.
  • Check who they really are. Search their name online with the word “scam”.
  • Use reverse image search on their photo to see if it appears elsewhere online.
  • Be careful what you share. Personal details can be used to steal your identity.
  • Do not send private or intimate photos or videos. Scammers may use them to threaten or blackmail you.
  • Do not keep the relationship secret. Talk to a trusted friend or family member.
  • Be cautious with investment offers, especially cryptocurrency. These are often fake.

Remember

Many people look online for friendship and love. That is normal. But scams are very common, and scammers are good at pretending to care. If someone you only know online asks for money, personal information, or secrecy, this is a major warning sign.

Think you have been scammed?

Act quickly to prevent more loss.

  • Contact your bank or card provider immediately. Ask them to stop any transactions.
  • Change your passwords on banking, email, shopping, and other important accounts.
  • Stop all contact with the person.
  • Report and block the profile on the website or app you used.

Who to contact

  • Action Fraud: Report online fraud or if you have lost money.
  • Your bank: If you sent money or shared banking details.
  • A trusted friend or family member: For support and help checking what to do next.
Feeling embarrassed or upset?

Please remember: scams are designed to trick people. Anyone can be targeted. It is not your fault. Reporting it can help protect you and other people.

Quick safety checklist

Do not send money. Do not share personal details. Do not keep it secret. Speak to someone you trust and check the person carefully.
