Mail Scams
Scam letters contain fake claims or offers designed to con you out of money. You can’t control what arrives — but you can control your response.

What to watch out for

  • Lottery/prize letters asking for an “admin fee”, purchase, or premium-rate call to claim winnings.
  • Bills from companies you don’t use (don’t use contact details printed on the bill).
  • Psychics/clairvoyants claiming they’ve seen your future.
  • Investment schemes asking you to pay and recruit others to get a return.
  • Requests for money due to “illness or poverty”.
  • Solicitor letters about an “unclaimed inheritance” (often overseas).

What to do

  • Reject: ignore it and throw it away. Never reply.
  • Report: join the Scam Marshal scheme (Friends Against Scams).
  • Ignore: don’t call premium-rate lines starting with 09 (can cost up to £4/min).
  • Verify: check organisation details using trusted sources.
  • Opt out: when registering to vote, opt out of the edited/open register.
  • Reduce: register with the Mailing Preference Service (MPS).
Quick bill safety check
  • If unsure you have an account, find official details in a phone book/online.
  • Do not use contact details printed on the suspicious bill.

Who to contact

  • Royal Mail: send to FREEPOST SCAM MAIL or email scam.mail@royalmail.com
  • Citizens Advice Consumer Service: report overseas scams and get advice.
  • SRA: check if a solicitor/law firm is genuine (NI: Law Society of NI).
  • MPS: reduce unsolicited sales/marketing post.
If you’ve lost money
  • Bank: call 159 or number on your card to freeze accounts/cancel cards.
  • Report: Action Fraud for a crime reference number.
