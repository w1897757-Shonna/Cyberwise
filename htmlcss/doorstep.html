Doorstep Scams
Someone comes to your door pretending to be trustworthy to get money, valuables, or access to your home.

What to watch out for

  • “Traders” claiming there’s something wrong with your property.
  • Fake police or bank staff asking for your PIN or cards.
  • Pushy sellers offering big discounts or time-limited deals.
  • Utility workers without proper ID.
  • Deliveries you didn’t order.
  • Fake charity collectors.
  • People asking to come inside for help.
What to do: You don’t have to open the door to anyone you don’t know.

Stop • Lock • Chain • Check

  • Stop: Are you expecting anyone?
  • Lock: Lock other doors first.
  • Chain: Use a chain and check safely.
  • Check: Ask for ID and verify it yourself.
More tips to avoid doorstep scams
  • Never buy from doorstep sellers.
  • Use “No cold callers” signs.
  • Set passwords with utility companies.
  • Don’t sign anything immediately.
  • Don’t accept unknown deliveries.
  • Never give away valuables or bank details.
  • If it sounds too good, it probably is.

Who to contact

Emergency: call 999

Non-emergency: call 101

  • Citizens Advice: for doorstep scams.
  • Action Fraud: report scams.
  • Check supplier: use official numbers.
  • Check charity: verify registration.
Feeling shaken is normal

Scams can affect confidence. Talking to someone and reporting it can help.
