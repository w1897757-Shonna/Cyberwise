Buying and Selling Scams
Learn how to spot fake sellers, fake buyers, scam websites, and unsafe payments.

Buying and selling scams happen when criminals pretend to be real buyers, sellers, shops, landlords, or service providers. They try to steal your money, bank details, or personal information.

What happens?

  • A scammer pretends to be a real seller and offers goods at a very low price.
  • A fake website or fake social media shop is made to look real.
  • A scammer may pretend to be a buyer and send fake payment messages.
  • They may ask you to pay quickly before you have checked the item or seller.
  • Once you pay, the item never arrives or the money is stolen.

Warning signs

  • The price is far lower than normal.
  • The offer sounds too good to be true.
  • The website has no clear contact details, returns policy, or terms and conditions.
  • A buyer agrees to pay without seeing the item first.
  • A buyer sends too much money and asks for some back.
  • You are asked to pay by bank transfer, gift card, crypto, or another unusual method.
  • The account name does not match the person or business you think you are paying.

How to stay safe

  • Stop and check before paying.
  • Use the official website of the business, not links from messages or adverts.
  • Read reviews carefully and be cautious if they all look the same.
  • Check that the business has real contact details and a real address.
  • Inspect expensive items in person before paying.
  • Use safer payment methods offered by trusted platforms.
  • Keep your messages inside the selling platform when possible.

How to check a website

  • Look closely at the web address for spelling mistakes or strange symbols.
  • Search the shop or website name online together with the word “scam” or “review”.
  • Check whether the site has a contact page, returns policy, and privacy information.
  • Be careful if the website was only created recently.
  • If the website does not feel right, do not enter your details.

Common types of buying and selling scams

  • Fake online shopping websites selling clothes, phones, or electronics.
  • Marketplace scams on Facebook, Gumtree, or similar sites.
  • Rental scams asking for a deposit before you view the property.
  • Pet scams for puppies or other animals that do not exist.
  • Health product scams selling unsafe or fake treatments.
  • Fake betting, gambling, or “investment-like” offers promising easy returns.

If you think you have been scammed

  • Contact your bank or card provider straight away.
  • Ask them to stop or review any payment.
  • Change your passwords for email, banking, shopping, and other important accounts.
  • Stop contact with the scammer.
  • Keep screenshots, receipts, messages, and emails as evidence.

UK help and support

  • Report fraud: Action Fraud – 0300 123 2040
  • Consumer help: Citizens Advice – 0808 223 1133
  • Report bad traders: Citizens Advice can pass reports to Trading Standards
  • Report fake websites: National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC)
  • Simple rule: Stop, Check, Then Pay
