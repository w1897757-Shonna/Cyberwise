Key message: Never trust unexpected calls asking for money, personal details, or urgent action.

What you learned

  • Scammers may pretend to be from your bank, the police, HMRC, or a delivery company.
  • They often sound urgent and try to make you panic.
  • They may ask for bank details, PINs, passwords, or payment.
  • You should hang up and contact the organisation using an official number.
Example scam scenario

You receive a call from someone claiming to be from your bank. They say there is suspicious activity on your account and ask you to confirm your card number, PIN, or one-time code. This is a scam. A real bank will never ask for your full PIN or password over the phone.

What to do if you get a suspicious call
  • Hang up straight away.
  • Do not share personal or banking information.
  • Do not press buttons or follow recorded instructions.
  • Wait a few minutes, then call the organisation back using an official phone number.
  • Tell a trusted family member or friend.
  • Report the scam if needed.
