Investment & Pension Scams
Scammers try to steal your pension or savings with “attractive” offers and pressure tactics.

What to watch out for

  • Any cold calls about your pension.
  • Offers of a “loan”, “savings advance” or “cashback” from your pension.
  • High-return investments in wine, jewellery, carbon credits or land; pressure to act quickly.
  • “Pension reviews” or early access before 55 for a fee (often via transfers).
  • Legitimate products sold as overvalued (little/no resale value).
  • Fee-to-join schemes where you must recruit others to profit.
  • Cryptocurrency/FX offers (often not regulated; protections may not apply).

What to do

  • Stay calm: don’t feel rushed or pressured.
  • Don’t commit: seek advice before deciding.
  • Stop the call: if pressured, end the conversation.
  • Think about foreign fraud: be wary of overseas firms avoiding regulations.
  • Check adverts carefully: celebrity endorsements can be faked.
  • Listen to your doubts: if it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.

Who to contact

  • FCA: check authorisation (Firm Checker) and ScamSmart warnings.
  • Pension Wise (MoneyHelper): independent pension guidance.
  • Independent adviser: via MoneyHelper or Personal Finance Society.
  • ASA: report online scam adverts.
  • Financial Ombudsman Service: escalate complaints if unhappy with how your bank handled a scam report.
