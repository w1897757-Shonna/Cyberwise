Key message: Be careful of unexpected pension or investment offers that promise quick profits, early access, or pressure you to act fast.

What you learned

  • Scammers may cold call about pensions or “special” investment opportunities.
  • They often promise high returns with little risk.
  • They may offer early pension access, loans, cashback, or savings advances for a fee.
  • Some scams involve cryptocurrency, foreign exchange, land, wine, jewellery, or carbon credits.
  • You should never make rushed financial decisions under pressure.
Example scam scenario

You receive a cold call from someone claiming to be a pension expert. They say they can help you unlock your pension early or move it into a high-return investment. They pressure you to act quickly and promise large profits with little risk. Later, you discover the company is not genuine and your money is gone. This is a common investment and pension scam.

How to protect yourself
  • Hang up on unexpected calls about pensions or investments.
  • Do not agree to anything immediately.
  • Check if the company is authorised before taking action.
  • Get independent advice before moving your pension or savings.
  • Be cautious of adverts using celebrity endorsements or urgent language.
  • If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.
