Business Email Scam
Learn how scammers trick you into sending money to the wrong account — and how to spot fake emails and websites before it is too late.

A business email scam is when a criminal pretends to be a company you trust and asks you to send money to the wrong bank account. Scammers also use fake websites and emails pretending to be banks, HMRC, or other trusted organisations to steal your details or money.

What happens in a business email scam?

  • You get an email that looks like it is from a real business.
  • The message may ask you to pay an invoice or deposit.
  • The bank details have been secretly changed by the scammer.
  • If you pay, your money goes straight to the scammer.

What to watch out for

  • You get a bill for something you did not order.
  • A company suddenly changes its bank details by email.
  • The email address looks slightly wrong or different from usual.
  • The message tells you to act quickly or urgently.
  • The payment request feels unusual or unexpected.
  • Fake websites that look like a bank, utility, or HMRC site and ask for your details.
  • Emails from abroad asking for money.
  • Unexpected attachments that may contain viruses.
  • Fake HMRC refund emails asking you to enter your details.
  • Fake invoice emails, including ones pretending to be from a solicitor.
  • Fake images, videos, or audio used to trick or blackmail.
  • Requests for one-time passwords (OTPs) when you are not logging in or making a transaction.

How to stay safe

  • Stop and check before sending any money.
  • Call the business using a phone number you already trust — not one from the email.
  • Look carefully at the sender's full email address.
  • Never rush a bank transfer.
  • Use a strong password for every account, especially email.
  • Do not click links in bank, HMRC, or utility emails — search for the site yourself.
  • Check the web address: look for https:// and a browser padlock.
  • Report and delete: forward scam emails to report@phishing.gov.uk, then delete.
  • Do not reply to suspicious emails — replying confirms your email address is active.
  • Filter junk: enable spam filtering in your email settings.
  • Stay virus-free: keep anti-virus and browser security features turned on.
  • Simple rule: Stop, Check, Call, Then Pay.

If you have been scammed

  • Contact your bank straight away and ask them to stop the payment.
  • Change your passwords for email, banking, and shopping accounts.
  • Report the fraud to Action Fraud.
  • Ask Citizens Advice for support if you need help.

UK help and support

  • Action Fraud: 0300 123 2040
  • Citizens Advice: 0808 223 1133
  • Get Safe Online: practical online safety advice.
  • Your email provider: use their reporting and security help pages.
If you have lost money
  • Bank: call 159 or the number on your card straight away.
  • Report: Action Fraud for a crime reference number.
