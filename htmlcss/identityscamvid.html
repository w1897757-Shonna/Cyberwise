Key message: Identity theft happens when someone steals your personal details and uses them to commit fraud in your name.

What you learned

  • Scammers may use your name, address, or bank details to open accounts or take out loans.
  • You may notice unknown transactions, missing post, or letters about accounts you did not open.
  • A sudden drop in your credit rating can be a warning sign.
  • Strong passwords, 2-factor authentication, and careful document disposal can help protect you.
  • Act quickly if you think your identity has been stolen.
Example scam scenario

You receive a letter about a phone contract you never signed up for. Then you check your bank account and see a payment you do not recognise. Later, you are refused credit because someone has already taken out financial products in your name. These are all possible signs of identity theft.

How to protect yourself
  • Keep your PINs, passwords, and bank details private.
  • Use strong passwords and do not reuse them across accounts.
  • Turn on 2-factor authentication where available.
  • Check your bank statements and accounts regularly.
  • Shred important documents before throwing them away.
  • Redirect your post when moving house.
  • Lock your phone and keep your software updated.
