Identity Theft
Your personal information is stolen and used to commit fraud (loans/credit goods) in your name.

What to watch out for

  • Unfamiliar activity or missing money from your bank accounts.
  • Post for someone you don’t know arriving at your address.
  • Unexpected credit rating changes (refused a loan etc.).
  • Told you’re already claiming benefits when you apply.
  • A phone contract set up in your name without your knowledge.
Good to know: 2-factor authentication (2FA) adds extra security to accounts.

What to do

  • Act quickly: cancel lost/stolen bank cards.
  • Double-check: if new cards don’t arrive, alert the organisation.
  • Watch your account: report unknown transactions.
  • Keep PINs/passwords safe: never share them.
  • Use strong passwords: avoid reuse.
  • Watch your cards: shield your PIN.
  • Stay safe online: keep software updated.
  • Phone security: lock your phone.
  • Bin carefully: shred documents.
  • When you move: redirect post.
After a death (bereavement safety)

Fraud can be committed using the identity of someone who has died. Use the Bereavement Register and the government’s Tell Us Once service.
