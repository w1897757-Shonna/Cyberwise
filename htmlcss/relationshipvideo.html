Key message: Be careful of anyone online who builds trust quickly, sends romantic messages, and then asks for money.

What you learned

  • Scammers may pretend to be a boyfriend, girlfriend, or close online partner.
  • They often send photos and ask for pictures back to build trust.
  • They may say they are abroad and need help because their bank account is blocked.
  • They use emotional pressure to ask for money, gifts, or personal details.
  • You should never send money to someone you have not met in real life.
Example scam scenario

You meet someone online who is kind, charming, and messages you every day. They send you lovely photos and ask for your picture too. After a short time, they say they care deeply about you and want to visit you from abroad. Then they tell you their bank account has been blocked and they need money for a flight, hotel, or emergency travel costs. They promise to pay you back. This is a common relationship scam.

How to protect yourself
  • Keep personal details private and do not overshare online.
  • Do not send money, gift cards, or bank details to online partners.
  • Be cautious if someone avoids video calls or meeting in person.
  • Search their name or photos online to check if they are fake.
  • Talk to a trusted friend or family member before making any decision.
  • Block and report the account if anything feels suspicious.
