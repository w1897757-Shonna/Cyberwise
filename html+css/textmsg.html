Text Messages Scams
Learn how to spot scam messages, stay safe, and get help in the UK.

Scam texts can look like they are from your bank, the government, a delivery company, or even someone you know. They often try to make you panic and click a link quickly.

What is a text scam?

  • You get a text message that looks real.
  • It may say there is a problem with your account, parcel, or payment.
  • It may ask you to click a link, call a number, or give personal details.
  • The message may be fake and designed to steal your money or information.

Warning signs

  • The message tells you to act immediately.
  • It asks you to click a link or call a number in the message.
  • It asks for passwords, bank details, or personal information.
  • It says your account has been hacked or your parcel cannot be delivered.
  • It threatens fines, charges, or loss of service if you do not act now.

How to stay safe

  • Do not click links in unexpected text messages.
  • Do not call the number written in the suspicious text.
  • Visit the official website yourself or use the official app.
  • Check with the organisation using a phone number you trust.
  • If a friend or family member says they have a new number, verify it first.

Common examples

  • Fake parcel delivery messages.
  • Fake bank security alerts.
  • Fake government payment or tax messages.
  • Messages saying a loved one has changed their phone number.
  • Job offers by text that sound too good to be true.

If you think you have been scammed

  • Contact your bank straight away.
  • Ask the bank to stop or review any payment.
  • Change your passwords for banking, email, and shopping accounts.
  • Report the scam and keep any messages as evidence.

UK help and support

  • Forward scam texts to: 7726
  • Report fraud: Action Fraud – 0300 123 2040
  • Advice and support: Citizens Advice – 0808 223 1133
  • Simple rule: Stop, Check, Delete
