Phone Scams
Be wary of unexpected calls or texts. You can always hang up if someone makes you uncomfortable.

What to watch out for

  • Calls “from your bank” or “the police” about fraud asking for your PIN or telling you to give your card to a courier.
  • “Fraud squad” calls asking you to help with an investigation.
  • Tech-support calls saying your device has a virus and asking you to download software (spyware).
  • Cold calls/texts offering pension or debt products/services.
  • Calls about Council Tax band/rebates out of the blue.
  • Calls asking you to pay to renew TPS (TPS is free).
  • Texts with links to “fix your account” or “track a parcel”.
  • Texts from a “family member” needing money urgently (new number story).

What to do

  • Say no: genuine organisations won’t ask for your PIN or say you must act immediately.
  • Report texts: forward to 7726 (free) to help your provider flag scam messages.
  • Check the line: scammers can keep the line open — use another phone, call someone you know first, or wait 10–15 minutes.
  • Use voicemail/answerphone: screen calls.
  • Caller ID: helpful, but scammers can appear “legitimate”.
  • Try call blocking: built-in features or a call blocker; some councils provide them.
  • Cut cold calls: join the free TPS (mobile too: text “TPS” + your email to 85095).
  • Avoid links: don’t click — contact the company via its official site/number.

Who to contact

  • Action Fraud: report scams.
  • Your bank: if worried, call 159 or number on your card.
  • TPS: register to reduce cold calls (free).
  • WhatsApp: open the chat → tap the name → “report contact”.
Extra help: call blockers

Ask your landline provider about call-blocking services (normally free). Some vulnerable people can access free blockers through professionals/councils.
